1. Introduction

The objective of the "Cube Craze" robot project is to design and build a robot that moves cubes around an arena in a fun competition where you face off against your classmates. The goal of each match is to gather more cubes than your opponent's robot by the end of a one-minute period.

Students will compete in teams of 3 (approximately 62 teams total). You will be provided with a kit of stock parts and access to fabrication facilities, along with a limited budget for additional components.

This document outlines rules of gameplay, team creation, robot constraints, manufacturing guidelines, competition logistics, deliverables, and grading.

3. Competition Rules and Overview

The competition includes a round-robin tournament followed by elimination rounds. Winners advance to compete against special robots built by ASML and TAs.

3.1 Gameplay

  • Each match lasts exactly one minute.
  • Robots must be fully autonomous.
  • No human interaction during matches.
  • Each side starts with 10 cubes.
  • Robots must start centered at the back of their zone.
  • No modifications allowed during competition.

3.2 Playing Field

The field is a 4’x4’ board divided into colored zones with a black border. Cubes are 1-inch wooden blocks.

3.3 Scoring

  • Cubes must be fully inside the robot perimeter to count.
  • Cubes shared between robots do not count.
  • Most cubes wins.
  • Ties in elimination rounds require rematches.

4. Teams

  • Teams of 3 students.
  • Signup via Canvas.
  • Team contract required.
  • Options available for students needing teams.

5. Robot Constraints

5.1 Dimensions

  • Start: within 8"x8"
  • During match: must fit within 12" diameter

5.2 Code

  • Must run on Arduino UNO
  • No Arduino commands (except limited exceptions)
  • Written in C using registers

5.3 Strategy

Robots must have a clear, justified design strategy.

5.4 Motors

  • Must use provided servo motors for wheels
  • External H-bridge required

5.5 Power

  • Limited to 4 AA batteries and 1 9V battery

5.6 Reusability

  • No permanent modifications to parts
  • Must allow disassembly

5.7 Safety

  • No weapons, fire, blades, or dangerous mechanisms
  • No projectiles or harmful materials

6. Equipment, Purchasing, and Fabrication

6.1 Provided Components

  • Sensors, motors, chassis, electronics

6.2 Free Components

  • Wires, resistors, LEDs, etc.

6.3 Batteries

  • Initial + replacement sets provided

6.4 Part Orders

  • $40 budget limit
  • No personal purchases allowed
  • Orders via Canvas

6.5 Lab Usage

  • Must reserve time slots
  • Max 3 per week
  • Strict attendance rules

6.6 Working Outside Lab

  • Allowed at home
  • Restricted spaces prohibited

6.7 Fabrication

  • Machine shop access (limited)
  • Laser cutting and 3D printing available

6.8 Penalties

  • Rule violations may result in disqualification
  • Tool misuse results in grade penalties

7. Competition Day

7.1 Overview

  • Round robin + elimination
  • Finals are best 2 out of 3

7.2 Logistics

  • Location: Duffield Atrium
  • Time: 10:00am–1:00pm
  • At least one team member must be present

7.2.1 Check-In

  • Robot inspection required
  • Code must be verified
  • No changes allowed after check-in

7.2.2 Returning Equipment

  • Return all parts after competition
  • $500 fee for missing equipment

8. Milestones

8.1 Seeding

Teams are ranked based on milestone completion order.

8.2 Milestone 1: Design Pitch

5-minute presentation with visuals.

8.3 Milestone 2: Mobility

  • Forward/backward movement
  • Turning
  • Path completion

8.4 Milestone 3: Sensors

  • Detect color
  • Detect borders
  • Autonomous reaction

8.5 Milestone 4: Cube Clearing

  • Gather at least 5 cubes in 1 minute
